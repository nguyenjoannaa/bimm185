Prescription Drugs

What are prescription drugs?

Prescription drugs are medications prescribed by the doctor. Improper use can be considered dangerous to the body. Commonly abused prescription drugs are opioids, central nervous system depressants, stimulants, and dextromethorphan (DXM)


How is it abused?

It is considered prescription drug abuse when the user is taking a medication without a prescription, in a way other than what is prescribed, and to get high. People abuse prescription for many reasons. Some of the reasons may be to obtain the high feeling, to relieve pain, or to help with school work.


What are the health risks?

Although prescription drugs are medications prescribed by doctors,, abusing them is not any safer than abusing illicit drugs. Using stimulant prescribed drugs can cause paranoia, high body temperature, and an irregular heartbeat. The use of depressants can cause speech to be slurred, shallow breathing, disorientation, or a lack of coordination. Abusing drugs that contain DXM can increase heart rate and blood pressure, nausea, and cause motor functions to be impaired. The abuse of any kind of medications can also lead to addiction and dependence.

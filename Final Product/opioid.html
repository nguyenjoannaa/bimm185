Opioids

What are opioids?

Opioids are pain relieving medications derived from the opium poppy plant. They work by binding to opioid receptors in the brain, spinal cord to reduce pain signals to the brain. .


How is it abused?

Although opioids are used for pain relief, the effects of using them can result in dependence and addiction. They are commonly abused due to the fact that they are easily accessible and their effects can provide a false sense of security.


What are the health risks?

The more opioids are used, the more tolerant the user gets to it. This causes the user to need to take more in order to get the same effect. Under the influence of opioids, the user can feel drowsy, constipated, nauseous, and confused. When taken in large doses, it may cause sleepiness, and can slow down the heart and breathing rate. Excessive use of opioids at once can cause breathing to become dangerously slow and shallow and may fill lungs up with fluid. Some people get unconscious or die due to the stopping of breathing. In the long term, opioids don't seem to have much effect other than causing the user to become dependent on it.

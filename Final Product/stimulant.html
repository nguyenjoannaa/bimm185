Stimulant

What are stimulants?

Stimulants are drugs that increase alertness for a short amount of time. When taken, it makes the user feel both mentally and physically less tired. Common socially accepted stimulants are nicotine and caffeine.


How is it abused?

The abuse of stimulants is often found in the method of binge use. Stimulants obtained through illegitimate means are often taken orally, sniffed, smoked or injected. Users develop a tolerance and addiction to stimulants and often times need to increase their dosage to receive the same high. Those who are heavily dependent on the substance may take the substance every few hours to satisfy their cravings.


What are the health risks?

The use of stimulants can lead to an increase of dopamine level which can induce a feeling of euphoria when taken for a non-medical reason. Taking stimulants may also constrict blood vessels,and increase blood sugar.

As with most drug abuse, the abuse of stimulants can result in dependence and addiction. Long term abuse can lead to paranoia or psychosis. Taking large doses may also lead to high body temperature and irregular heartbeat along with risk for cardiovascular failure.

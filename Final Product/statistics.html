Did You Know?

Just about everyone knows that the legal drinking age throughout the United States is 21. But according to the National Center on Addiction and Substance Abuse, almost 80% of high school students have tried alcohol. (http://kidshealth.org/teen/drug_alcohol/alcohol/alcohol.html)

Teen drinkers are more likely to get fat or have health problems, too. One study by the University of Washington found that people who regularly had five or more drinks in a row starting at age 13 were much more likely to be overweight or have high blood pressure by age 24 than their nondrinking peers. (http://kidshealth.org/teen/drug_alcohol/alcohol/alcohol.html)

In 2003, 31 percent of drivers age 15 to 20 who died in traffic accidents had been drinking alcohol (http://www.alcoholscreening.org/Learn-More.aspx?topicID=8&articleID=25)

Binge drinkers are 14 times more likely to report alcohol-impaired driving than non-binge drinkers. (http://www.cdc.gov/alcohol/fact-sheets/binge-drinking.htm) About 90% of the alcohol consumed by youth under the age of 21 in the United States is in the form of binge drinks. (http://www.cdc.gov/alcohol/fact-sheets/binge-drinking.htm)

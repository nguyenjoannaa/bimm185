Cannabinoid

What are cannabinoids?

Cannabinoids are compounds found in the plant Cannabis Sativa. There are seven subclasses of cannabinoids classified based on their degree of psychoactivity.


How is it abused?

Marijuana is the most commonly abused illegal drug in the United States. Although some may say that it is not possible to abuse marijuana, people have become dependent and addicted to it.


What are the health risks?

Cannabinoids affect the user by interacting with receptors in different areas of the central nervous system (CNS). The effects of the interactions depend on which part of the brain they are interacting with. Interactions with the limbic system shows an effect on memory, cognition and psychomotor performance whereas interactions with the mesolimbic pathway associated with feelings of rewards. The effects of using marijuana can include distorted perceptions, impaired coordination, difficulty in thinking and problem solving, and learning and memory problems.

Hallucinogens

What are hallucinogens?

Hallucinogens are drugs that distorts the way the user sees reality. Under it's influence, the user will see, feel, and hear things that don't exist. It works by distrupting the interactions between one's nerve cells and neurotransmitter serotonin and the brain and spinal cord.


How is it abused?

Depending on the person taking hallucinogens, the effects are very variable and unreliable. Because they are so unpredictable, using hallucinogens can be considered dangerous to use. The abuse of the drugs are for the hallucinogenic experience.

LSD is usually taken orally for the "trips" that last for around twelve hours. Peyote is typically prepared in a tea due to the bitter extract and it's effects can also last for twelve hours. The effects of psilocybin take place within twenty minutes of consumption and can last up to six hours. Phencyclidine can give the user a trip that lasts between four to six hours.


What are the health risks?

Under the influence of hallucinogens, the user will experience anxiety, fear, and paranoia. In the long run, hallucinogens put the user at risk of developing long-lasting psychoses or mental disorders.

Club Drugs

What are club drugs?

Club drugs are psychoactive drugs commonly abused by teenagers and young adults. They tend to be colorless, odorless, and tasteless.


How is it abused?

Club drugs are commonly found used at bars, nightclubs, concerts, parties, and raves. Because they are colorless, odorless, and tasteless, GHB and Rohypnol for example, this allows for them to slipped into an unsuspecting victim's drink making it unable for them to resist sexual assault.


What are the health risks?

Effects that are common to all club drugs include anxiety, panic, depression, euphoria, loss of memory, hallucinations, and psychotic behavior. However, taking stimulant club drugs such as MDMA and methamphetamine could also result in increased heart rate, convolusion, rise in body temperature, uncontrollable movements, insomnia, impaired speech, dehydration, high blood pressure, and grinding of the teeth. Taking sedative/hallucinogenic drugs like GHD, LSD, and Rohypnol can result in slow breathing, decreased heartrate, respiratory problems, intoxication, drowsiness, confusion, tremors, and nausea

Types of club drugs

1. Methylenedioxymethamphetamine (MDMA)

2. Gamma-hydoxybutyrate (GHB)

3. Methamphetamine

4. Ketamine

5. Rohypnol

6. Lysergic Acid Diethylamide (LSD)
